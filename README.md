## 📊 How It Works

Pingio sends small packets of data to test your connection quality. It analyzes the path your data takes to reach the internet. The app shows these results using clear charts.

- Ping: This measures the delay between your computer and a server. Lower numbers indicate better response times.
- Jitter: This measures the variation in your ping. Low jitter helps with video calls and gaming.
- Download Speed: This shows how fast you receive data from the internet.
- Upload Speed: This shows how fast you send data to the internet.

These metrics update continuously as you use the application. You see changing values as your network traffic fluctuates.

## 🛠️ Troubleshooting

If you encounter issues, check these frequent solutions:

- Permission Errors: If the app fails to start, restart your computer and try again.
- Connection Errors: Make sure you have a working internet connection before you start a test.
- Blank Charts: If charts stay empty, check if your firewall or antivirus restricts network access for the app.
- Slow Tests: If tests take too long, restart your router to refresh your local network state.

The application operates as a standalone tool. It does not store your private data on external servers. All information remains on your local machine.

## 📈 Understanding Your Results

Your internet service provider defines your baseline speed. Your actual speed differs based on your hardware and home setup. Use Pingio to check if your results match the speeds you pay for. 

Testing over a wired connection usually provides more accuracy than using Wi-Fi. Wi-Fi signals face interference from walls and other household devices. If your results seem low, try moving closer to your router or using an Ethernet cable.

## 🚀 Tips for Better Tests

Use these tips to get the most accurate information from your speed test:

- Stop current downloads: Turn off updates or large file downloads during your test.
- Close unused tabs: Browsers and streaming apps consume bandwidth. Close them to ensure accurate readings.
- Test multiple times: Run tests at different times of the day to see if your speed changes.
- Use a stable connection: Keep your computer near the router to reduce signal loss.

The internal diagnostics run locally. This ensures your privacy while you track your connection stability throughout the day. You will find that regular monitoring helps you identify clear patterns in your internet performance.
